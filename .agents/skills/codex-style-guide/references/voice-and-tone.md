# Voice and tone

Source: <https://doc.wikimedia.org/codex/latest/style-guide/voice-and-tone.html>

Good UI content keeps users focused on their task and lets them decide
quickly and confidently.

## Voice (consistent, who we are)

- We are the **voice of free knowledge** — keep the user focused on the
  task they're doing.
- **Neutral point of view.** Avoid personal opinions in how we address
  users.
- Be clear and concise without getting complicated; consistent without
  feeling mechanical; directive while always respectful.

## Point of view — who speaks, to whom

- **First person (I/we):** generally avoid, except when it clearly
  refers to the Foundation or a Wikimedia entity ("We are in this
  mission together").
- **Second person (you/your):** use for instructing the user. Prefer
  **"Your"** over **"My"** in feature names — "Check your events page",
  not "Check your 'My Events' page".
- **Third person, active voice:** use for content and most UI copy
  ("They received a message").

## Tone (shifts with the task)

The tone adapts to context while the voice stays constant:

- **Warnings / errors:** plain, clear, neutral — don't alarm.
- **Success / completion:** celebratory and informative.

Always match the severity of the message; never make a routine notice
read like a crisis.
